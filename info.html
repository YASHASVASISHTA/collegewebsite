Exam Details and Structure

JEE (Main) Exam (Latest Pattern)

Details
Purpose Admission to undergraduate engineering programs at NITs, IIITs, other centrally funded technical institutions, and state-level colleges. It is also a qualifying exam for JEE Advanced.
Exam Mode Computer-based Test (CBT), except for the Drawing section in Paper 2A (B.Arch) which is offline (Pen-and-Paper).
Exam Duration 3 hours (180 minutes) for all papers (Paper 1, Paper 2A, Paper 2B).
Marking Scheme
  • MCQs (Section A): +4 for each correct answer, -1 for each incorrect answer.
  • Numerical Value Questions (NVQs) (Section B): +4 for each correct answer. **Note: Official guidelines for 2026 indicate a negative marking of -1 for incorrect answers in NVQs as well. Check official bulletin.**
Subjects & Questions (Paper 1 - B.E./B.Tech)
  • **Total Questions:** 75 (25 questions each from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics).
  • **Structure (Per Subject):** 20 MCQs (Multiple Choice) and 5 NVQs (Numerical Value Questions).
  • **Attempt:** All 75 questions are mandatory (No choice in Section B).

NEET (UG) Exam

Details
Purpose Admission to undergraduate medical (MBBS), dental (BDS), and AYUSH (BAMS, BUMS, BHMS, etc.) courses in India.
Exam Mode Offline (Pen-and-paper mode).
Exam Duration 3 hours 20 minutes (200 minutes).
Marking Scheme
  • +4 for each correct answer.
  • -1 for each incorrect answer.
  • 0 for unattempted questions.
Subjects & Questions 200 questions total, out of which 180 must be attempted. Divided into Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (Botany & Zoology). Each subject has Section A (35 compulsory Qs) and Section B (15 Qs, attempt any 10).

KCET Exam

Details
Purpose Admission to undergraduate courses in Engineering, Technology, B. Pharma, B. Arch, and other professional programs in colleges across Karnataka.
Exam Mode Offline (Pen-and-paper mode).
Exam Duration 80 minutes for each subject paper (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics/Biology).
Marking Scheme
  • +1 for each correct answer.
  • There is **no negative marking** for incorrect answers.
  • No marks will be awarded for multiple answers to a single question.
Subjects & Questions 180 questions total (60 questions each from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics). Each subject paper is worth 60 marks.
